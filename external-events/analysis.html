1.Overview & the so-what

synvert xgeeks, a GlobalLogic Company, is a driving force in the Portuguese tech ecosystem. We found, organize and operate communities ourselves, we are not only sponsors, investing our people's time, our spaces and our resources. That work groups into four engines that run all year.

  • Meetups, the always-on engine. Year-round presence across six cities and six-plus tech domains. Historically about brand, exposure and talent; over the last year, also a live business pipeline.
  • geekathon, our hackathon. Our hackathon in Leiria, external by design and popular internally, and in 2026 the proposed platform for GlobalLogic and Hitachi group synergies.
  • KCD Porto, the flagship. The largest CNCF event in Portugal. In 2026 it runs together with DevOpsDays Portugal for a 500+ attendee landmark event.
  • Recognition, the external stages. Through its community leadership, xgeeks holds standing roles at Portugal's biggest tech conferences (JNation, SREday, LLMday), at essentially zero cost.
The so-what. Community is no longer just a brand and talent play. It is a self-sustaining engine that also produces a real business pipeline (~750K EUR) and earns xgeeks recognition on the largest tech stages in Portugal. This document lays out where each engine is and where it is heading, so marketing can amplify it.

3.Community as a business engine

Community-building always served business, talent and brand. About a year ago we started working meetups deliberately as a lead-generation channel. Today our fully operated and co-organized meetups actively generate qualified business leads. The mechanism is simple and repeatable:

  1. Curate. We screen the check-in lists before each event, identifying potential leads among registrants (companies, roles, seniority, relevance to our services).
  2. Connect. During the event, our team makes direct, personal contact with those leads in a community-first setting. No cold calls, no cold emails.
  3. Nurture. Because prospects already know and trust the community, follow-up after the event lands warm, not cold.
  4. Expand. The database grows from recurring meetups plus larger events (KCD Porto, geekathon, partner conferences), each reaching different audiences and seniority levels.

Prospects have already seen xgeeks organize, host and contribute, so conversations start from a place of trust. Repeated touchpoints with the same prospects shorten sales cycles, and our organizers are often the first to spot an opportunity.

Proven results. Business leads originated from community include CloudZone, Wellhub and UBIO, with more continuing to emerge. Current community-driven pipeline value is approximately 750K EUR. This validates community as a genuine, scalable business development engine, not just a brand and talent pillar.

4.Self-sustaining meetups

The community has reached a point where the ecosystem comes to us. Companies actively want to provide venues, host events and sponsor them, for the exposure to our audience. Budget is rarely the constraint anymore.

  • Venues offered. Companies want to host our meetups in their spaces.
  • Hosts and speakers. Partners volunteer to co-host and bring speakers, placing their brand alongside ours.
  • Inbound sponsorship. Sponsors approach us to fund events.
The signal. When demand outpaces the calendar, the hardest part is saying no. That is the clearest sign the engine is self-sustaining.

6.Co-organizing & partnerships

A clear trend in the calendar: fewer solo meetups, more joint editions with other meetup groups. Co-organizing means bigger audiences, shared reach and lower cost per event, because each partner brings their own audience, speakers and visibility. Our community work also keeps attracting major tech players who want to partner with us.

6.1 Google

A partnership with Google Developer Groups (GDG), with joint meetups in Braga, Porto and Lisbon in 2026. A national tech brand alongside ours, in three cities.

6.2 OutSystems

A growing, national-level collaboration with the OutSystems User Groups. Many partnerships have already happened in Viseu and Leiria, and it is now expanding to Porto: their VP of Community and Business Relations came to Leiria, and we are preparing something together in Porto in September. This is another proof point of how our community work attracts major tech players who want to partner with us.

Our reach extends across the entire ecosystem. Beyond the groups we own directly, we constantly run meetups in partnership between our groups and external ones. Our community presence extends well beyond what we own, through a constant web of collaborations across the country, all year.

7.geekathon

geekathon is our community-driven hackathon, held in Leiria and now a flagship tech event in the region. It has run since 2021 and grows edition after edition.

7.1 External by design, loved internally

geekathon was always conceived as an external event, and it works extremely well on both fronts. Because our own people genuinely love it, it naturally also became a strong internal moment that raises more and more interest inside the company, while continuing to grow externally with more partners edition after edition. It is external by design, loved internally, and growing on both fronts.

7.2 The 2025 edition

  • Theme: "Driving the Future with AI", with two challenge areas, Sustainable Mobility and Smart Factories.
  • Scale: 17 teams building AI solutions, close to 20 projects delivered, nearly 100 participants.
  • Organizers and partners: synvert xgeeks, Grupo Lusiaves, BRAINR, Startup Leiria and AWS, with recurring sponsors and 20+ community partners.
  • Prizes: Best Project (jury), Audience Award, and Sponsor Challenges. Winning teams in 2025 included Os Brunos (Best Project) and Pastel de Data (Audience Favourite).
  • Social component: 100% of ticket sales donated to a social cause, reinforcing the community-driven mission.
Data note. The internal source note and the public site describe the 2025 edition slightly differently (theme framing and exact date). Team count (17) and the two AI challenge areas are consistent across both, so those are used here.

8.geekathon 2026: a proposal to the group

This is a proposal, not a done deal. This year we would like to put geekathon on the table as a way to help build synergies across GlobalLogic, Hitachi Digital Solutions and the associated group companies. It simply makes sense: a great way for the group to understand what we do and how we do it (our business, our engineering, and our approach to community), and a way for them to show up and take part.

There are many possible formats, and we are open to explore. For example:

  • Sponsor prizes tied to challenges that solve their real problems, with solutions they could actually use afterwards.
  • Participate with their own teams.
  • Join the jury panel.
  • Other formats we can shape together.
The tagline. From Leiria to the Hitachi group, and from the Hitachi group to Leiria. Framed as an open invitation to co-build: a showcase of local community strength to the group, and a channel for the group to engage with the Portuguese tech ecosystem.

11.Recognition on national stages

Beyond the events we own and organize, our community leadership earns xgeeks recognition across the wider ecosystem. Through it, xgeeks holds standing roles at major external conferences, giving the brand presence and network access at essentially zero cost. This is a fourth engine alongside meetups, geekathon and KCD Porto.

11.1 JNation, Champion

xgeeks holds a Champion role at JNation, the biggest generalist tech conference in Portugal, held annually in Coimbra at Convento São Francisco with around 1,500 participants. It covers Java, JavaScript, Cloud & Infrastructure, AI/ML, Security and Methodologies, with talks, workshops and deep dives from international experts. The 2026 edition (26-27 May) sold out. The Champion role is a recognized community-advocate position: representing and amplifying the conference within the community.

11.2 SREday & LLMday, Ambassador

xgeeks holds an Ambassador role for SREday and LLMday, international conference series (US, Europe, Asia) run by the team behind sreday.com, llmday.com, devopsnotdead.com and conf42.com. SREday focuses on reliability, DevOps and cloud (35+ events, 10+ countries, 4,100+ attendees to date); LLMday focuses on LLMs, AI and ML. Smaller than JNation, but international brands with strong technical communities. They currently run once a year in Portugal, in Lisbon (hosted at PagerDuty), and are now expanding to Porto as well, with xgeeks helping make the Porto expansion happen. The Ambassador role includes helping bring conferences to new cities and building the local community.

The takeaway. xgeeks is recognized by the wider ecosystem, not just self-declared. That translates into presence, credibility and network access on the largest and most international tech stages in Portugal, without owning or funding those events.

12.In conclusion: one flexible channel

The goal at community level is a single, national, always-on channel with real plasticity: one channel that can carry whatever we need it to. It has already proven itself for brand and talent, and now it works for business too.

  • Brand, proven. Consistent, organic presence across six cities and the tech ecosystem, all year.
  • Talent, proven. Our most authentic employer-branding channel, where engineers meet engineers.
  • Business, now. A real, community-driven pipeline of ~750K EUR, with named leads and more emerging.

Because it is a national audience that we reach directly and repeatedly, it is also a natural carrier for transversal campaigns and content, and not only for those. That flexibility is the point: the channel exists, it works, and it can carry more.

In one line. A community channel for transversal campaigns, and not only. Proven for brand and talent, now delivering business too.
