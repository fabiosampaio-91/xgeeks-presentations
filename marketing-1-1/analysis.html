0.The headline to land

The single most important angle of this 1-1 is business. Everything else supports it.

If she remembers one thing. I built a community engine with almost no financial investment, and it is now a real, measurable business channel that I am actively working on. Brand, talent and community are the foundation that made the business channel possible.

Delivery notes: confident, direct and data-driven, but humble and collaborative on the marketing-help topic. We want to learn from her team and get their help to scale this to the next level. Keep it a conversation, not a pitch.

1.Who I am & top achievements

Question: Who are you professionally? Top achievements? Key projects you manage?

Say it like this: I am Fábio, Business & Community Specialist at synvert xgeeks, a GlobalLogic Company. My foundation and identity is community: I own our external-facing brand through events, our external events strategy, and all communication for and with the community. At this moment I also carry business responsibility, working closely with André. The best way to frame it is community driven for business: the community work is my core, and it is now deliberately being leveraged as a business channel. The logic was simple: community was already delivering strong results in branding, and we were seeing organic inbound just from our name being present at meetups and events. So we decided to leverage everything we had built, the database, the tracking, the events, the meetups, and turn it into a business development channel.

1.1 Top achievement: KCD Porto

I brought the largest cloud native conference format to Portugal. We did such a strong job that in 2026 DevOpsDays Portugal decided to join forces with us (KCD Porto x DevOpsDays Portugal 2026), and the event is growing from ~330 to 500+ attendees. It reached break even, we have full control of it, and it opens opportunities across talent, brand and business.

1.2 Second achievement: the community footprint

I started in 2023 with 2 meetup groups. Today I run more than 10 at national level, with presence in the North, Center and Lisbon, across cloud, DevOps, engineering leadership and AI. That footprint sits inside a wider ecosystem of 17 groups reaching roughly 24,000 members. Organically, xgeeks became known as a community builder in the Portuguese tech scene. What makes me proud is that all of this was built with very little financial investment.

2.1 Where this is heading: this team owns the commercial relationship

A clear direction is emerging from the accounts we are already winning. The initial customer relationship and the commercial layer should sit with this team, so it stays consistent and owned, rather than spread across the organization. As we win more accounts, we structure ownership by account, with a clear owner per account, so it scales cleanly, and it keeps engineering leadership focused on delivery rather than account management. Part of my role now is helping the wider company understand and adopt this operating model.

3.What I do in-country

Question: What do you actually do in-country?

Say it like this: Most of my work is fully in-country: the national meetup network; KCD Porto as our flagship; geekathon, which I now also run as Project Manager; the business development that comes out of all of it; and growth marketing and lead attraction on top. The community footprint is our in-country presence. The one exception is the newest responsibility, supporting synvert group synergies with Hitachi (alongside Soares), which is group-level rather than local.

4.Group / regional marketing

Question: What do you do for group marketing / regional marketing?

Say it like this: My contribution to regional marketing is external events and visibility. Everything I do puts the xgeeks brand physically in front of the Portuguese tech community, consistently, across cities and tech domains. It is marketing through presence and community leadership rather than through campaigns.

4.1 KCD Porto, a group-level asset

The clearest group-marketing point is KCD Porto: the event we organize and will run again, with massive brand visibility and strong business potential. It has already been presented to Wolfgang, who was interested and wants to scale it even further in the coming years. That makes it not just a national event, but a platform the group wants to grow.

4.2 geekathon group synergies, a proposal

There is also a group angle I would like to propose with geekathon: making it an aggregator event for the group, creating synergies with GlobalLogic, Hitachi Digital Solutions and the associated companies, with their teams participating in challenges and prizes. From Leiria to the Hitachi group, and from the Hitachi group to Leiria.

Frame it as a proposal. The geekathon group angle is an open invitation to co-build, not a confirmed plan. Possible formats: sponsor prizes tied to challenges that solve their real problems, participate with their own teams, join the jury, and other formats we can shape together.

4.3 Supporting synvert group synergies with Hitachi

Beyond the events, I am also helping with synergies at the synvert group level with Hitachi, working with Soares. It is an ongoing support role at group level and the newest part of my scope, distinct from the in-country community and events work.

5.Aspiration & superpower

Question: What is your aspiration and superpower?

Say it like this: My superpower is people. I can handle a large number of people, partners and stakeholders at the same time, and I am people driven in the sense that I always try to align everyone's interests so that expectations are met on both sides, xgeeks and our partners. I would describe my role as a mediator and diplomat between all these parties, and a network builder. Creating and expanding networks is what I do naturally, and it is exactly the skill that turned our community into a business channel.

6.Growth vs Talent

Question: What do you do for Growth vs Talent, specifically?

Say it like this: It is community as a whole, everything we do in external events, that feeds both Growth and Talent: meetups, KCD Porto, geekathon, partnerships and the database, all of it. On the talent side, it remains our most authentic employer-branding channel: people meet us, see how we work, and want to join. But right now my work is more focused on Growth: check-in curation before events, direct contact with potential leads during events, warm follow-up powered by community engagement, and a database fed by the full external events engine. This has already produced real leads (CloudZone, Wellhub, UBIO) and a pipeline of approximately 750K EUR. In my role map, this is two areas working together: business & sales (top of funnel) and growth marketing & lead attraction.

7.Vertical expertise

Question: What is your vertical / industry expertise?

Say it like this: Community, Events, and Growth and Sales. That is my vertical. Within that, deep domain familiarity with the cloud native, DevOps and AI ecosystems in Portugal, since those are the communities I run.

Operationally it costs xgeeks zero euros, it is break even and fully under our control, and if we leverage it well the return is significant: at minimum, the creation and expansion of the network of decision-makers that people inside xgeeks can activate for business.
