## 1. Dual-role state matrix (BD rule for every sponsor state)

| State (2026) | BD allowed? | Hand-off pattern | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Closed** | Yes — green light | A (Fábio intro) or C (on-floor) | Confident peer; separate business chat |
| **In negotiation** | Not yet — wait til signed | Hold; B once on track | Protect the deal |
| **Said NO this year** | Yes, carefully | A or B, time-separated | Gracious; "a different conversation" |
| **No reply this year** | Yes, soft | B (Sales/peer); A if prior relationship | Low-key, value-first |
| **Past sponsor, now quiet** | Yes — warmest re-open | A (Fábio reconnects) → B | "Great working together in '24/'25…" |
| **Never sponsored (cold)** | Only if fit + a route-in | B, or partner/community referral | Pure peer/community |

## 2. The hand-off — double opt-in, never cold

Ask both sides first · forwardable blurb ("I'll let you two take it from here") · separate the intro
in time from any renewal ask.

- **Pattern A — Fábio intro, then steps back.** Soft, peer ("you two should compare notes on
  platform/cloud"). Works even on a no-reply / polite no where prior contact exists.
- **Pattern B — direct peer outreach (default).** **Ivan (Sales)** or a manager reaches the route-in
  directly, leading with the KCD/community tie — never "Fábio gave me your email."
- **Pattern C — on-the-floor.** Fábio can give intros & walk people to booths; **Ivan pre-curates a
  check-in list** (sponsors + attendees). A manager talks shop; business emerges. Fábio = host.
