## 0. The "so what" (30 seconds)

We don't just sponsor KCD Porto — **we are the team behind it**. That gives xgeeks warm
relationships with **60+ cloud-native companies**, the **speakers**, and the **bulk-ticket buyers**
(Siemens, NOS, …) — exactly the orgs that buy nearshore cloud, platform, data and AI engineering.
**~€59K closed in 2025** (~€33K in 2024), ~20 returning sponsors.

**Recommendation:** a **dual-track** motion — Fábio keeps closing sponsorships; a separate xgeeks
business voice opens commercial conversations across **many channels (before / during / after)**,
governed by a per-account **state matrix**, **double-opt-in hand-offs**, and a shared **BD CRM**.
Dinners are one door among many. Measure on **€ pipeline influenced over 6–12 months at ≥4:1**.

Plain language: **ABM** = pick a short list of high-value companies, treat each as its own
mini-campaign. **Pipeline influenced, not lead volume** = € of real conversations/deals opened, not
contact counts. **Slot into the gaps** = time BD for moments that don't clash with the sponsorship
or the event. **Target** = signs/holds budget. **Route-in** = the warm path. **Double opt-in** =
never connect two people cold; ask both first.

## 4. The dual-track rule, the state matrix & the hand-off

Rule: sponsorship first; never pay-to-play (either direction); separate the voices (Fábio =
organizer; a different voice opens business).

**State matrix — BD rule for every sponsor state:**
| State (2026) | BD allowed? | Pattern | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Closed | Yes — green light | A or C | Confident peer; separate business chat |
| In negotiation | Not yet — wait til signed | B later | Protect the deal |
| Said NO this year | Yes, carefully | A/B, time-separated | Gracious, "different conversation" |
| No reply this year | Yes, soft | B (Sales/peer) | Low-key, value-first |
| Past sponsor, now quiet | Yes — warmest re-open | A → B | "Great working together in '24/'25…" |
| Never sponsored | Only if fit + route-in | B / referral | Pure peer/community |

**Hand-off — double opt-in, never cold** (ask both sides, forwardable blurb, time-separated):
- **A · Fábio intro, steps back** — soft, peer ("you two should compare notes on platform/cloud"),
  works even on a no-reply/no where prior contact exists.
- **B · Direct peer outreach (default)** — **Ivan (Sales)** or a manager reaches the route-in,
  leading with the KCD/community tie (not "Fábio gave me your email").
- **C · On-the-floor** — Fábio can give intros & walk people to booths; **Ivan pre-curates a
  check-in list** (sponsors + attendees); a manager talks shop, business emerges. Fábio = host.

## 5. Channel playbook — the doors (KCD is one door, not the only one)

### Before — remote & ahead-of-time (biggest window)
- **Email & LinkedIn** (most important) — peer message opened with **"we're the team behind KCD
  Porto"**, soft transition to "could we help with X". One reusable template across sponsors,
  speakers & ticket buyers.
- **The existing logistics relationship** — we already talk to sponsors constantly; business opens
  off it once a sponsorship is closed, via the separate voice, never inside a logistics email.
- **Bulk-ticket buyers** (Siemens, NOS) — Fábio hands each to **Ivan (Sales)** for a KCD-hook →
  exploratory-call sequence.
- **Past speakers (2024–25 DB)** — many at top cloud/AI firms; reach out ahead on their work +
  community. Also feeds 2026 CFP & meetup speakers.

### During — on-site, peer-to-peer
- **Booth (first-class)** — staff with sales + eng managers; walk to sponsor booths; curiosity first.
- **Venue lunches & cocktails** — natural low-pressure surfaces; Ivan's check-in list guides who to
  find. (Dinners are off-site.)
- **Speaker dinner (Day 1 or 2)** + **guest dinner** — invite **all sponsors + a few curated
  speakers + a few of our own happy clients ("faz bonito")**, aiming for senior signatories.
- **The bench (deploy by strength):** openers/connectors **Luís Soares, André, Helder Sancho, Ivan
  (Sales)**; deep-technical **Gui** (likely KCD speaker), **Gonçalo**, + senior engineers (Tiago,
  Serra, Ricardo, Oliveira, Bessa, Sousa). "Let me grab Gui" hand-off. No assigned seating, no scripts.

### Year-round — national meetups (Fábio can own)
We run many meetups Braga→Lisbon. Fábio runs this as low-pressure warming with easy hooks ("looking
for speakers / a venue") across ex-/current/prospective sponsors, past speakers, and ticket buyers.
(We do NOT run "DevOps Days PT".)

## 6. How to introduce the business topic (copy-pasteable)

- **Before — email/LinkedIn:** *"Hi [name] — I'm with the team behind KCD Porto, great to have
  [company] involved again. Wearing my other hat: I'm on the engineering side at xgeeks and we do a
  lot of nearshore platform/cloud work. Saw you're scaling [X] — worth a 20-min swap of notes before
  November? No agenda, just peers."*
- **Before — bulk-ticket buyer / speaker (via Ivan):** *"From the KCD Porto team. Since your folks
  are joining, thought I'd reach out from the xgeeks engineering side — we help teams like yours
  scale delivery. Quick exploratory call?"*
- **During — booth:** *"What are you building these days? … We run a lot of that nearshore — how's
  the platform side holding up as you scale?"*
- **During — dinner:** openers build rapport; when it turns technical, *"let me grab Gui — he's deep
  in exactly this."*

**Never:** imply sponsoring buys business (or vice-versa); sell from the stage; pitch a competitor.
