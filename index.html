005 · The state matrixThe hand-off
THE HAND-OFF DOUBLE OPT-IN, NEVER COLD
synvert xgeeks006 / 018

The warm-intro best practice that stops it feeling pushy

Three ways to pass the baton.

Pattern A · Fábio intro, steps back
Fábio asks both sides if they're open, then connects with a 1–2 line blurb on each and "I'll let you two take it from here." Framed soft & peer — "you two should compare notes on platform/cloud", never "here's Ivan, let's do business." Works even on a no-reply or a polite no, where prior contact exists.
Pattern B · Direct peer outreach (default)
Ivan (Sales) or a manager messages the route-in directly, leading with the KCD/community tie — not "Fábio gave me your email." De-awkward it: the relationship is KCD, the topic is engineering, the ask is a peer chat.
Pattern C · On-the-floor
The most natural. Fábio can give intros and physically walk people to booths; Ivan pre-curates a check-in list of sponsors & attendees to seek out. A manager talks shop; business emerges. Fábio is host, not salesperson.

Double opt-in = never connect two people cold; the introducer first checks both sides are happy, then connects. Always separate the intro in time from any sponsorship/renewal ask, so it never feels quid-pro-quo. Route-in = warm path; Target = signs / holds budget.

013 · During the eventThe language
THE LANGUAGE COPY-PASTEABLE WORDING
synvert xgeeks014 / 018

Real messages for each setting — remote first

How to introduce the business topic.

Before · email / LinkedIn (reusable)
"Hi [name] — I'm with the team behind KCD Porto, great to have [company] involved again. Wearing my other hat: I'm on the engineering side at xgeeks and we do a lot of nearshore platform/cloud work. Saw you're scaling [X] — worth a 20-min swap of notes before November? No agenda, just peers."
Before · bulk-ticket buyer / speaker (via Ivan)
"Hi [name] — from the KCD Porto team. Since your folks are joining, thought I'd reach out from the xgeeks engineering side — we help teams like yours scale delivery. Quick exploratory call?"
During · booth (ours / theirs)
"What are you folks building these days? … We run a lot of that nearshore — how's the platform side holding up as you scale?" Curiosity first; let the need surface.
During · dinner — match the person
Soares/André/Helder open & build rapport; when it turns technical, "let me grab Gui — he's deep in exactly this"; Gui/Gonçalo go deep and, where it fits, suggest a follow-up.
Never
Imply sponsoring buys business (or vice-versa); sell from the stage; pitch a competitor.
