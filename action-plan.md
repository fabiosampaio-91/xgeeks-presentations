## 4. The dual-track rule (this protects both jobs at once)

We wear two hats with the same people. They must never collide.

1. **Sequencing / do-no-harm.** Sponsorship comes FIRST. Secure/protect the 2026
   sponsorship, *then* open the business conversation — separated in time and, where
   possible, through a different person/channel. Never open BD with an account mid-negotiation.
2. **Never pay-to-play, either direction.** Sponsoring does not buy xgeeks business; doing
   business does not buy sponsorship perks or any on-program/stage advantage. KCD stays
   vendor-neutral; the brand is host, not hero.
3. **Separate personas/channels.** The organizer voice (sponsorship asks) is distinct from
   the xgeeks business voice (commercial conversations). See track owners in the private
   playbook.
4. **Parallel tracks, no conflict.** Per account we map: sponsorship stage, the right moment
   to introduce the business topic relative to that stage, and who owns each track.
   Returning sponsors who are already comfortable are the warmest for BD — **but only once
   their 2026 sponsorship is secure or clearly on track.**

**Green-light rule:** BD track opens only when sponsorship status is **CLOSED** or clearly
on track. Already green for 2026: MC Sonae, Critical TechWorks, AWS (+ Komodor, CLEVA as
partner/neutral). Everyone in active negotiation stays Track-A-only until signed.

## 5. Channel playbook (multi-channel — KCD is one door, not the only one)

### 5.1 Email outreach / nurture
- **First touch is a peer continuation, not a cold pitch.** Reference the real shared
  context (KCD, a talk they gave, a topic from their booth). Offer a concrete reason to
  talk: a platform/architecture benchmark, a roadmap review, the dinner invite.
- Cadence: touch 1 (peer/relevance) → touch 2 +4 days (value: a relevant write-up) → touch 3
  +1 week (soft ask: a 20-min peer conversation) → nurture if no reply.
- The business theme arrives as *"we kept thinking about what you said about X"*, never
  *"want to buy engineering services?"*.

### 5.2 Campaigns / ABM / LinkedIn / content
- Targeted ABM campaigns to Tier-1/2 accounts; thought-leadership on platform engineering,
  cloud-native team scaling, GPU/AI workloads on Kubernetes.
- LinkedIn: warm the route-in and target with relevant content before any meeting; engage
  authentically (their posts, their talks). Pre-event content warming measurably lifts
  on-site meeting acceptance.

### 5.3 Community & meetups (developer-community-led, value-first)
- Kubernetes / cloud-native meetups, AWS UG, DevOps Days PT — show up as engineers, give
  talks, help the community. This is relationship capital that pays off over 6–18 months and
  keeps xgeeks credible and non-salesy.

### 5.4 KCD & event motion — the dinners are the core asset
- **Speaker dinner** — peers, speakers, partners. Thought-leadership and partner/referral
  relationships (AWS, Komodor, Red Hat, etc.).
- **Guest dinner** — curated Tier-1 decision-makers. Every seat maps to a target account.
- **Guest list IS the product:** seniors attend based on who else is in the room. No filler.
  Frame each dinner around a narrow theme ("platform engineering at scale," "AI/GPU workloads
  on Kubernetes," "scaling cloud-native teams").
- **Seating is a chess game:** place each target decision-maker beside (a) an xgeeks person
  who speaks their language and (b) a happy current client for organic social proof.
- **Run-of-show:** one moderator, one topic, no hard selling, a discussion guide with planted
  prompts to surface capacity/roadmap pain. Brand is host, not hero.
- **Capture:** for each named account log attendee, tier, topic, signals, next step → CRM
  within 24h.

### 5.5 Partner co-sell (AWS anchor)
- AWS is a closed Gold sponsor and a strategic xgeeks partner with an **internal route (Davi
  Garcia)**. Run a co-sell motion: xgeeks as AWS delivery partner into joint accounts.
  Partner referrals bypass sales objections and shorten cycles.

## 6. How to introduce the business topic (relationship → need → conversation)

The framing is **peers in engineering discovering a need**, not seller → buyer.

- **Opening (relationship):** *"Loved your team's talk on the catalog-to-Kubernetes
  migration — we run a lot of those nearshore. How's the platform side holding up as you
  scale?"*
- **Discovery (need):** listen for capacity gaps, roadmap pressure, hiring pain, delivery
  risk. *"Where are you most capacity-constrained right now?"*
- **Bridge (conversation):** *"That's exactly the kind of thing our teams pick up — want me
  to set up a 20-minute call with one of our platform leads, totally exploratory?"*

**Red lines (KCD community / vendor-neutral):** off-stage relationship-building only; never
trade stage/agenda/program for commercial gain; never imply sponsorship ↔ business
reciprocity; respect the CNCF Code of Conduct and attendee privacy at all times.
